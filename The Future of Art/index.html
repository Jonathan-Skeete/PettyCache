The Origin of Art

Thousands of years ago, a man drew his own shadow; he took up a burnt stick and drew around it. He started to draw stick figures in the caves. This is how art got started. This legend refers to caveman drawings that are more than 30,000 years old. People then did not see it as art, but today we see drawing as a form of art. Most historians agree that if art had one place for where it started, it would be in Africa; African people who lived more than 70,000 years ago have drawn art and art objects.Art is one of those things that does not have a specific date or time when it started. Since the beginning of time, human beings have had this natural creative ability. Whether it was the caveman carving into the stones of a cave or someone wanting to sculpt or mold a lump of rock into something beautiful, we have wanted to find a way to create art since the beginning of time.

Leonardo Davinci

The Future of Art

Opinion 1

The art of the future will be transformed by holographic technology. Museums and galleries will display stunning 3D holographic works that seem to float in mid-air without any screens or supports. Holographic art will range from intricate sculptures that visitors can walk around and view from all angles to massive immersive installations that envelop the viewer in surreal dreamscapes of light and motion. Artists will create holographic living paintings that subtly shift and evolve over time. The imagery will change based on the viewer's perspective, adapting itself in real time to look different from various angles. Abstract geometrical shapes will kaleidoscopically transform as people interact with the artwork. Outdoor holographic art will also become popular. Cities could have entire holographic districts where fantastical alien landscapes, psychedelic visions, and surreal cityscapes are projected onto buildings and spill out onto the streets. Holographic creatures and characters will roam these urban wonderlands, playing and interacting with delighted tourist. Performance art will be reinvented by holograms. Holographic actors will star in avant-garde holographic theatre productions. Musicians could perform as volumetric holograms on stage, transforming in appearance during a concert. Holographic ballerinas and acrobats will amaze audiences with supernatural feats. The art of the future will break free of physical constraints and open portals to new dimensions using holograms. Artists will guide viewers into impossible worlds where the imagination can roam freely. Holographic art will profoundly change how people connect with art and alter the very notion of reality.

Opinion 2

Technology has evolved into one of the main powerhouses of the world, so too has Art. Art has transitioned throughout time beginning wih cavemen drawing their shadows to the vibrant and expressive forms we see today. Generally, there is no distinctive description of what art will look like in the future due to the constant evolution and fast paced changes of our world, but I do think these evolutions in technology will play a big part in how art will be constructed and presented. I believe art will develop into a digital realm that will provide unique experiences not only for the developer but also its viewers. It will be focused on a more visual and intangible form more so than traditional paintings or drawings. AI's will play a big role in how it is presented and provide experiences for viewers that would be easily accessible along with a vibrant and clear cut imaging.
